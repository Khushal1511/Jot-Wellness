Families often begin therapy with hope, but the day-to-day experience can quickly become confusing when every professional gives advice from a separate lens.

At JOT Wellness, coordination means one shared roadmap across behavior support, speech therapy, occupational therapy, special education, school needs, and parent coaching.

## Why coordination matters

A child does not experience life in therapy categories. Communication, sensory regulation, attention, sleep, learning, play, and family routines all affect one another.

When the team works together, parents receive:

- One priority list instead of competing recommendations
- Clear home strategies that fit real routines
- Progress reviews that connect therapy goals to family life
- Fewer repeated assessments and fewer mixed messages

## What parents can look for

Ask whether your child's team is reviewing goals together, sharing observations, and measuring outcomes that matter at home and school.

> The goal is not more therapy. The goal is better-aligned support.

Small coordinated changes can make routines calmer, communication clearer, and progress easier to sustain.
