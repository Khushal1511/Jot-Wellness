Many parents wait anxiously for words. Words are important, but communication starts before speech.

A child may communicate through eye gaze, reaching, pulling a parent by the hand, sounds, gestures, facial expressions, movement, or behavior. These signals deserve attention.

## Support the message first

Before asking a child to say a word, notice what they are trying to express:

1. Are they requesting something?
2. Are they avoiding discomfort?
3. Are they asking for help?
4. Are they trying to share joy?

When adults respond to the message, children learn that communication works.

## Make communication easier

Offer choices, pause longer, use simple language, and celebrate attempts. If a child needs visuals or alternative communication, that is not a failure. It is a bridge.

Speech therapy is strongest when it protects connection while building skills.
